Privacy and Our Access to Your Content Good ▶

This section details how Adobe accesses and uses your content. Key points include:

  • No Scanning of Local Content: Adobe explicitly states it does not scan or review content stored locally on your device. This is a significant consumer-friendly protection.
  • Cloud Content Review: Content uploaded to Adobe's servers (Cloud Content) may be automatically scanned for illegal or abusive material (e.g., Child Sexual Abuse Material). If flagged, human review may occur. This is standard practice for platform safety.
  • Content Analytics: Adobe may perform analytics on Cloud Content to improve services and provide recommendations, with an explicit opt-out option for users.
  • Public/Shared Content: Content shared publicly (e.g., Adobe Stock, Behance) is subject to review for intellectual property and safety issues.

Overall, Adobe provides commendable transparency and limitations on its access to your content, especially distinguishing between local and cloud data and offering opt-out for analytics.

Generative AI Content Usage Good ▶

This is a particularly strong and consumer-friendly clause. Adobe explicitly states: "We will not use your Local or Cloud Content to train generative AI models except for Content you choose to submit to the Adobe Stock marketplace."

This means your personal projects and files, whether stored locally or in the cloud, will not be used to train Adobe's AI models unless you specifically contribute them to a commercial platform like Adobe Stock, which is governed by a separate agreement. This provides significant protection against common industry practices that have raised privacy and intellectual property concerns for users.
