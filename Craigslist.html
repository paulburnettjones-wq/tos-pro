Craigslist Terms of Use Analysis

Changes to Terms & Incorporation of Policies Bad ▶

This clause states that Craigslist may modify the Terms of Use at any time in their sole discretion. Your continued use of the service after changes means you are bound by them. This is a common but highly unfavorable clause, as it allows the company to unilaterally alter the agreement without requiring your explicit consent to the new terms.

It also incorporates several other policies (like the Privacy Policy and Prohibited List) by reference, meaning you need to read multiple documents to understand the full scope of your agreement.

User Content License Bad ▶

When you post content on Craigslist, you grant them a 'perpetual, irrevocable, unlimited, worldwide, fully paid/sublicensable license to use, copy, display, distribute, and make derivative works from content you post.'

This is an extremely broad license. It means Craigslist can use anything you post, anywhere in the world, forever, for any purpose, and even sublicense it to others, without paying you or needing your further permission. This is a significant surrender of rights over your own content.

Prohibited Use & Content Moderation Concerning ▶

This section outlines numerous prohibited actions, including:

  • Using unauthorized software (e.g., scrapers, bots).
  • Copying/collecting CL content via automated or manual means.
  • Posting misleading, unsolicited, or unlawful content.
  • Collecting user information or interfering with CL.

Crucially, Craigslist reserves the right to moderate access and use 'in our sole discretion,' which includes blocking, filtering, deleting, delaying, or terminating your access/license/account. They explicitly state they are 'not liable for moderating or not moderating.' This gives Craigslist immense power over your content and access to the service, with no accountability for their moderation decisions, which could be arbitrary or unfair.

Liquidated Damages for Violations Critical ▶

This is a highly aggressive and unusual clause. It sets specific, often substantial, monetary penalties (liquidated damages) for various violations of the 'USE' section. Examples include:

  • Collecting user information: $1 per violation.
  • Publishing third-party personal info without consent: $1,000 per violation.
  • Posting Prohibited Content: $4 per violation.
  • Sending unauthorized emails/texts: $25 to $500 per violation.
  • Aggregating/exploiting CL content: $3,000 for each day of violation.
  • Accessing more than 1,000 pages in 24 hours: $0.25 per page after the first 1,000.

Craigslist claims these amounts are a 'reasonable estimate of our damages' and 'not a penalty,' but also states they don't limit their ability to recover under any other legal theory, including statutory damages or equitable relief. This clause places a significant and explicit financial risk on users for even minor or accidental breaches of the terms, which is far beyond what is typically seen in consumer-facing ToS.

Fees & Refunds Concerning ▶

This clause states that fees for paid postings are non-refundable, even if Craigslist removes, delays, omits, re-categorizes, re-ranks, or otherwise moderates your post. This means you pay for a service, but Craigslist retains the right to prevent your content from being displayed as intended, without returning your money. This shifts all risk of moderation or service issues onto the user.

Disclaimers of Warranties & Limitation of Liability Critical ▶

This clause is extremely broad and unfavorable to users:

  • 'AS IS' and 'AS AVAILABLE': Craigslist provides its service without any promises, warranties, or representations regarding its completeness, accuracy, availability, security, or reliability. You assume all risk of using the service.
  • Disclaimer of all warranties: They disclaim all warranties, express or implied, including merchantability, fitness for a particular purpose, and non-infringement.
  • Extreme Limitation of Liability: Craigslist Entities (including officers, directors, employees) are NOT liable for any direct, indirect, consequential, incidental, special, punitive, or other losses (including lost profits, data, goodwill) arising from or related to the service. Furthermore, any liability is capped at $100 or the amount you paid in the preceding year, whichever is less.

This means if anything goes wrong with the service, or if you suffer damages due to its use, Craigslist takes virtually no responsibility, and your potential recovery is severely limited to a negligible amount.

The Craigslist Terms of Use document presents a highly consumer-unfriendly legal framework. The overarching theme is one of extreme control and minimal liability for Craigslist, while placing significant burdens and risks on the user.

Key concerns include the unilateral right to modify terms at any time, the demand for a perpetual, irrevocable, and broad license to all user-posted content, and extensive rights to moderate content and user access at their sole discretion with no liability. The most egregious clauses are the Liquidated Damages for Violations, which impose specific, often substantial, financial penalties for a wide range of user actions, and the Disclaimer of Warranties and severe Limitation of Liability, which effectively absolves Craigslist of almost all responsibility for service failures or user damages, capping any potential liability at a mere $100.

Consumers using Craigslist should be aware that they are operating under terms that offer very little protection or recourse. The company reserves the right to take almost any action regarding content or user accounts without accountability, and users face significant financial risk for perceived violations. This ToS is a stark reminder for users to exercise extreme caution when interacting with the platform and its content.
