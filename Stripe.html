Mandatory Arbitration and Class Action Waiver Bad ▶

Content: Section 13 of the General Terms mandates individual binding arbitration for any disputes between you and Stripe. It also includes a class action waiver.

Impact: This clause significantly limits your legal recourse. You waive your right to sue Stripe in court and participate in a class action lawsuit. Arbitration is often less transparent and can favor the company.

Consumer Impact: If you have a dispute with Stripe, you must go through arbitration, which can be costly and time-consuming. The class action waiver prevents you from joining forces with other users to pursue a collective claim, even if many users have similar grievances.

Consumer Impact: You may need to adapt your business processes to accommodate changes Stripe makes to its services. The lack of control over service features can be problematic if Stripe removes a critical function.
